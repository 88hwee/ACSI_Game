Stalin’s Mood Board – Events & Explanations

Read the Cold War event, then explain Stalin’s reaction in your own words.

Instructions

  1. Read the event and short explanation in each row.
  2. Think about Stalin’s aims, fears and priorities at this time.
  3. In the last column, type a short explanation (2–3 sentences) of how and why Stalin would react.
Cold War Event What happened? Explain Stalin’s reaction
Iron Curtain Speech (1946) Churchill gives a speech saying an “iron curtain” has fallen across Europe, accusing the USSR of controlling Eastern Europe and spreading communism.
Poland under Soviet Control After the war, a communist government takes power in Poland with Soviet backing, even though the USSR had agreed at Yalta that there would be free elections.
COMINFORM (1947) Stalin creates Cominform, an organisation to coordinate and direct communist parties in Europe from Moscow.
Marshall Plan (1947) The USA offers billions of dollars to help European countries rebuild their economies. The plan is open to all European states, including those in Eastern Europe.
Czechoslovak Coup (1948) Communists, supported by the USSR, remove non-communist leaders in Czechoslovakia and take full control of the government.
Berlin Blockade (1948–49) The Western Allies introduce a new currency in their zones of Germany and in West Berlin. In response, Stalin blocks all land and water routes to West Berlin to try to force them out.
Berlin Airlift Succeeds The USA and Britain organise an airlift, flying supplies into West Berlin for almost a year. The blockade fails and Stalin eventually reopens the routes.
NATO Formed (1949) The USA, Britain and other Western countries form NATO, a military alliance promising to defend one another if attacked.
COMECON (1949) The USSR sets up Comecon to organise economic cooperation between communist countries and link their economies more closely to the Soviet Union.
USSR Tests A-bomb (1949) The Soviet Union successfully tests its first atomic bomb, ending the period when only the USA had nuclear weapons.
