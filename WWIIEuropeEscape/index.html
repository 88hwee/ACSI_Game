Lebensraum made war more likely because it required Germany to expand beyond its borders to gain land and resources, particularly in Western Europe. This expansion could be achieved peacefully through agreements and cooperation. As Germany pursued this goal, it mainly improved relationships with neighbouring nations, reducing tensions. Combined with Hitler’s willingness to use force, lebensraum turned ambition into action, making conflict almost inevitable.

Corrected Version

Lebensraum required expansion into Eastern Europe, could not be achieved peacefully, and increased tensions.
